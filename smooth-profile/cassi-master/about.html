My Background

Robotics Club to Computer Nerd

If someone were to tell me what lied ahead in the computer science field during my senior year of high school, I would have been overwhelmed as to how people can create and achieve so much. Compared to other computer science students, I was a late bloomer in terms of having any experience with programming. In high school, I was part of the Robotics club where we made rough phases of apps via block-coding. When I wasn't staying after school to play around with apps, I would be at home tinkering with old PCs. Initially, I had entered college for computer engineering. However, the major didn't click well with me, so I shifted my focus onto cybersecurity after taking part of IT bootcamp. There, I was amazed about how quick it is for outsiders to take information from people. Training against cybersecurity attacks is sometimes offered at workplaces, but not everyone chooses to be aware of their tracks online or don't think it is that important. I want to be able to work with servers and utilize cybersecurity protocols to maintain the integrity of a client or company's computer systems. As a growing cybersecurity major, my goals are to continue down the path of IT certifications, work in New York State for a few years to develop field experience in IT and cybersecurity, and then move elsewhere to either work for another company or pursue a teaching position in computer science.
